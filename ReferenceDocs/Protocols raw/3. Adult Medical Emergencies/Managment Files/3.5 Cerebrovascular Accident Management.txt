Cerebrovascular Accident (Stroke)
protocolid= 22
=============================
EMTP/EMTI
---------------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Perform general patient management. Obtain time of symptom onset.
<tr><td>2.<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation. Be alert for aspiration, upper airway obstruction and hypoventilation.
<tr><td>3.<td>Administer oxygen to maintain <i><u>SPO</u></i><sub>2</sub> 94 - 99%. Support respirations as necessary with a BVM.
<tr><td>4.<td>Place patient in position of comfort.
<tr><td>5.<td>Transport rapidly, but carefully. Notify the receiving hospital as early as possible. Scene time should be less than 20 minutes (See Stroke Triage Plan).
<tr><td>6.<td>Perform rapid glucose determination. If glucose less than 60 mg / dL or clinical signs and symptoms indicate hypoglycemia, refer to the <i><u>Hypoglycemia protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>7.<td>Perform and document CINCINNATI PREHOSPITAL STROKE SCALE evaluation.
<tr><td>8.<td>Establish an IV of normal saline at KVO.
<tr><td>9.<td>Place patient on cardiac monitor and obtain/interpret <i><u>12 lead ECG</u></i>.
<tr><td>10.<td>Perform ongoing assessment as indicated. IMPORTANT: Ensure that a witness accompanies the patient to the hospital or a contact telephone number for the witness is secured for the hospital.
<tr><td>11.<td>Transport to closest appropriate hospital with capabilities to provide the appropriate level of treatment (see Stroke Triage Plan).
