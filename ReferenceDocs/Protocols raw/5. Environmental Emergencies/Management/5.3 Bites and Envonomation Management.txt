Bites and Envonomations
protocolid=48
===================================
EMTP/EMTI/EMTEN
--------------------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Perform general patient management.
<tr><td>2.<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3.<td>Treat for shock and conserve body heat. Keep the patient calm.
<tr><td>4.<td>If applicable, locate the fang marks and clean the site with soap and water. Note: There may be only one fang mark.
<tr><td>5.<td>Remove any rings, bracelets, or other constricting items on the bitten / stung extremity.
<tr><td>6.<td>Keep any bitten / stung extremities immobilized – the application of a splint will help. Keep the injury at the level of the heart. When not possible, keep the injury below the level of the heart.
<tr><td>7.<td>DO NOT apply light constricting bands above and below the wound.
<tr><td>8.<td>If envenomation is suspected, every 15 minutes, use a pen to mark the border of the advancing edema and document the time.
<tr><td>9.<td>Consult Medical Control or Poison Control. For serious envenomation, the patient may need to be transported or evacuated to a hospital with the appropriate anti-venom.
<tr><td>10.<td>Start an IV of Normal Saline at KVO.
<tr><td>11.<td>For signs and symptoms of shock, follow the <i><u>Shock protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>12.<td>Transport and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.
