Organophosphate Exposure
protocolid=66
=========================
EMTP/EMTI
----------------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Scene Safety and consider HAZ MAT activation.
<tr><td>2.<td>Ensure patient has been thoroughly decontaminated.
<tr><td>3.<td>Obtain general assessment of the patient.
<tr><td>4.<td>Administer Oxygen to maintain <i><u>SPO</u></i><sub>2</sub> 94 - 99%
<tr><td>5.<td>Suction oropharynx as necessary.
<tr><td>6.<td>Establish IV of Normal Saline. Titrate to maintain a systolic BP > 90 mmHg.
<tr><td>7.<td>Place the patient on a cardiac monitor and obtain / interpret <i><u>12 lead ECG</u></i>.
<tr><td>8.<td>If the patient has respiratory distress due to secretions, administer <i><u>ATROPINE SULFATE</u></i> 1 - 2 mg IVP every 5 minutes until signs of pulmonary secretions decrease improve or medication supply is exhausted. There is no maximum dose in this situation.
<tr><td>9.<td>If available, administer 2PAM 1 - 2 Gram IV one dose - <b>OR</b> - 0.6 Gram IM x 3 doses in rapid succession.
<tr><td>10.<td>If patient is seizing, refer to the <i><u>Medical Care Seizure protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>11.<td>For bronchospasm, administer <i><u>ALBUTEROL</u></i> and <i><u>ATROVENT</u></i> per <i><u>Medial Care: Respiratory Distress protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>12.<td>Transport promptly in position of comfort. Reassess vital signs as indicated.
