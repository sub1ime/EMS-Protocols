Hypoglycemia Peds

=====================
1. Perform general patient management.
2. Support life-threatening problems associated with
airway, breathing, and circulation.
3. Assess for signs of trauma. Provide spinal
immobilization as necessary.
4. Administer oxygen to maintain SPO 2 94 - 99%
5. For altered mental status, perform rapid glucose
determination.
6. If glucose < 60 mg / dL or clinical signs and symptoms
indicate hypoglycemia:
a. If the patient can protect airway, give Oral
Glucose15 grams. Repeat in 15 minutes if
necessary.
7. If glucose < 60 mg / dL or clinical signs and symptoms
indicate hypoglycemia and oral glucose is
contraindicated: Establish an IV of normal saline at
KVO
a. If the patient is < 30 days old, administer
DEXTROSE 10% (2 cc / kg) via IV or IO.
b. If the patient is > 30 days old, but < 8 years old,
administer DEXTROSE 25% (2 cc / kg) via IV or
IO.
c. If the patient is > 8 years old, administer
DEXTROSE 50% (0.5 mg / kg, max 25 mg) via
IV or IO.
d. If unable to establish an IV, alternatively
administer GLUCAGON
• Under 20 kg: 0.5 mg IM
• >20 kg 1 mg IM
8. For signs and symptoms of hypovolemic shock or
dehydration, follow the Pediatric Shock protocol.
9. Place on cardiac monitor per patient assessment.
10. Transport and perform ongoing assessment as
indicated.
