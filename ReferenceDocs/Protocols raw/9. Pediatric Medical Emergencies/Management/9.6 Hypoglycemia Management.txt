Hypoglycemia Peds
protocolid=79
=====================
EMTP/EMTI
-----------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Perform general patient management.
<tr><td>2.<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3.<td>Assess for signs of trauma. Provide spinal immobilization as necessary.
<tr><td>4.<td>Administer oxygen to maintain <i><u>SPO</u></i><sub>2</sub> 94 - 99%
<tr><td>5.<td>For altered mental status, perform rapid glucose determination.
<tr><td>6.<td>If glucose < 60 mg / dL or clinical signs and symptoms indicate hypoglycemia:
<tr><td>a.<td>If the patient can protect airway, give Oral Glucose15 grams. Repeat in 15 minutes if necessary.
<tr><td>7.<td>If glucose < 60 mg / dL or clinical signs and symptoms indicate hypoglycemia and oral glucose is contraindicated: Establish an IV of normal saline at KVO
<tr><td>a.<td>If the patient is < 30 days old, administer <i><u>DEXTROSE 10%</u></i> (2 cc / kg) via IV or IO.
<tr><td>b.<td>If the patient is > 30 days old, but < 8 years old, administer <i><u>DEXTROSE 25%</u></i> (2 cc / kg) via IV or IO.
<tr><td>c.<td>If the patient is > 8 years old, administer <i><u>DEXTROSE 50%</u></i> (0.5 mg / kg, max 25 mg) via IV or IO.
<tr><td>d.<td>If unable to establish an IV, alternatively administer <i><u>GLUCAGON</u></i>
• Under 20 kg: 0.5 mg IM
• >20 kg 1 mg IM
<tr><td>8.<td>For signs and symptoms of hypovolemic shock or dehydration, follow the <i><u>Pediatric Shock protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>9.<td>Place on cardiac monitor per patient assessment.
<tr><td>10.<td>Transport and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.
