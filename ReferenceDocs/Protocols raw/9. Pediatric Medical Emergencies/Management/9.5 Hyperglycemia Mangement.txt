Hyperglycemia - Peds
protocolid=78
===========================
EMTP/EMTI
-----------------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Perform general patient management.
<tr><td>2.<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3.<td>Assess for signs of trauma. Provide spinal immobilization as necessary.
<tr><td>4.<td>Administer oxygen to maintain <i><u>SPO</u></i><sub>2</sub> 94 - 99%
<tr><td>5.<td>For altered mental status, perform rapid glucose determination.
<tr><td>6.<td>If glucose > 300 mg / dL, start an IV of normal saline.
<tr><td>7.<td>For signs and symptoms of hypovolemic shock or dehydration, follow the <i><u>Pediatric Shock protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>8.<td>If glucose level is > 300 mg / dL, and no signs of shock are noted, administer maintenance Normal Saline infusion:
• 4.0 ml / kg for first 1 - 10 kg of weight.
• Add 2.0 ml / kg for next 11 - 20 kg of weight.
• Add 1.0 ml / kg, for every kg of weight, > 20 kg.
• Multiply total amount x 2= total hourly hyperglycemic maintenance amount.
<tr><td>9.<td>Place on cardiac monitor and obtain / interpret <i><u>12 lead ECG</u></i> per assessment.
<tr><td>10.<td>Transport and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.
