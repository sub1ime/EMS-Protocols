Head Injury
protocolid=39
=============================
EMTP/EMTI
-----------------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Perform general patient management .
<tr><td>2.<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3.<td>Administer oxygen to maintain <i><u>SPO</u></i><sub>2</sub> 94 - 99%. Consider supporting respirations with a BVM.
<tr><td>4.<td>Immobilize patient using full spinal precautions. Avoid excessive compression around the neck by cervical collar. Assess and document PMS in all extremities before and after immobilization.
<tr><td>5.<td>Obtain and document baseline GCS and reassessments.
<tr><td>6.<td>Monitor <i><u>capnography</u></i>, if available. Attempt to maintain between 35 - 45 Torr.
<tr><td>7.<td>Place patient on cardiac monitor.
<tr><td>8.<td>Establish an IV of normal saline at KVO. If time permits, establish an additional line.
<tr><td>9.<td>Obtain a blood glucose sample.
<tr><td>10.<td>If patient is exhibiting signs of shock, refer to <i><u>Shock protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>11.<td>Transport and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.
