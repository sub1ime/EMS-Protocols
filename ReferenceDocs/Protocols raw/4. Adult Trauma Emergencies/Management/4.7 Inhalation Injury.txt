EMTP/EMTI
-------------
<table><tr><td>1.<td>Perform general patient management.
<tr><td>2.<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3.<td>Administer oxygen to maintain <i><u>SPO</u></i><sub>2</sub> 94 - 99%. If indicated, support respirations with a BVM.
<tr><td>4.<td>Consider <i><u>orotracheal intubation</u></i> for impending respiratory failure.
<tr><td>5.<td>Monitor <i><u>capnography</u></i>, if available. Maintain a range between 35 - 45 Torr.
<tr><td>6.<td>Place patient on cardiac monitor and obtain/interpret <i><u>12 lead ECG</u></i>. Refer to appropriate <i><u>Cardiac Care protocol</u></i>.
<tr><td>7.<td>Establish an IV of normal saline at KVO.
<tr><td>8.<td>If carbon monoxide is suspected or confirmed, administer O<sub>2</sub> with non-rebreather mask.
<tr><td>9.<td>If cyanide is suspected or confirmed, and kit is available, administer CyanokitÂ®. **See administration instructions.
<tr><td>10.<td>If available, consider <i><u>CPAP</u></i> with 5 - 10 cm H<sub>2</sub>0 PEEP.
<tr><td>11.<td>Transport to Burn Center and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.
