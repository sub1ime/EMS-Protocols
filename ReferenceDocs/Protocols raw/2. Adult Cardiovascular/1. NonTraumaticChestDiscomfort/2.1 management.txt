<table><tr><td>1<td>Perform general patient management.
<tr><td>2<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3<td>Treat dysrhythmias. Be prepared to initiate CPR and defibrillation, if necessary.
<tr><td>4<td>Administer supplemental oxygen to maintain SPO 2 94 - 99%
<tr><td>5<td>Obtain patient history. Reassure the patient.
<tr><td>6<td>Place patient on cardiac monitor.
<tr><td>a. <td>Obtain a 12 lead ECG, as soon as possible.
<tr><td>b. <td>Consider ALS rendezvous, especially when the 12-lead indicates the patient is experiencing a STEMI.
<tr><td>c. <td>When a 12 lead ECG indicates “***ACUTE MI***” notify closest appropriate Emergency PCI center (cath hospital).
<tr><td>7<td>Transport as soon as feasible.
<tr><td>8<td>Administer ASPIRIN 324 mg to chew.
<tr><td>9<td>Administer NITROGLYCERIN.
<tr><td>a. <td>Assist patient with PRESCRIBED NITROGLYCERIN. If the pain persists and B/P > 100 mmHg systolic, repeat nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL in 3 to 5 minutes (up to total of three SL doses).
<tr><td>10<td>Transport to appropriate hospital. Patients with ECGs consistent with STEMI should be transported ONLY to PCI CAPABLE HOSPITALS.
<tr><td>11<td>Transport and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.

<table><tr><td>1<td>Perform general patient management.
<tr><td>2<td>Support life-threatening problems associated with airway, breathing, and circulation.
<tr><td>3<td>Treat dysrhythmias. Be prepared to initiate CPR and defibrillation, if necessary.
<tr><td>4<td>Administer supplemental oxygen to maintain SPO 2 94 - 99%
<tr><td>5<td>Obtain patient history. Reassure the patient.
<tr><td>6<td>Place patient on cardiac monitor.
<tr><td>a. <td>Obtain a 12 lead ECG, as soon as possible.
<tr><td>b. <td>When a 12 lead ECG indicates “***ACUTE MI***” notify closest appropriate Emergency PCI center (cath hospital).
<tr><td>7<td>Transport as soon as feasible.
<tr><td>8<td>Administer ASPIRIN 324 mg to chew.
<tr><td>9<td>Establish an IV of normal saline at KVO.
<tr><td>10<td>If history consistent with cocaine associated chest pain and 12 lead not indicative of STEMI, administer MIDAZOLAM 5 mg IV. Alternatively administer DIAZEPAM 2.5 - 5 mg IV. Skip to step 14
<tr><td>11<td>Administer NITROGLYCERIN.
<tr><td>a. <td>Assist patient with PRESCRIBED NITROGLYCERIN. If the pain persists and B/P > 100 mmHg systolic, repeat nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL in 3 to 5 minutes (up to total of three SL doses).
<tr><td>b. <td>Administer nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL. If the pain persists and B/P > 100 mmHg systolic, repeat nitroglycerin 0.4 mg SL in 3 to 5 minutes (up to total of three SL doses).
<tr><td>12<td>If pain persists following administration of nitroglycerin SL, apply one (1) inch of nitroglycerin paste.
<tr><td>13<td>If pain persists following administration of a minimum of 3 SL nitroglycerin and nitroglycerin paste, consider FENTANYL titrated to pain relief at 1 mcg / kg IV/IM, not to exceed 50 mcg per single dose. May repeat every 10 minutes. Alternatively, administer MORPHINE 0.1 mg / kg IV at 1 mg / min., not to exceed 10 mg, titrated to effect.
<tr><td>14<td>Transport to appropriate hospital. Patients with ECGs consistent with STEMI should be transported ONLY to PCI CAPABLE HOSPITALS.
<tr><td>15<td>Transport and perform ongoing assessment as indicated.
